# Growth Experiment Design Playbook

This playbook keeps low-verifiability marketing work honest by making hypotheses, evidence, and kill criteria explicit.

## Contract

- Treat growth ideas as experiments, not facts.
- Cite source evidence for audience, channel, keyword, or claim.
- Define a measurable success metric and a kill criterion before execution.
- Do not publish regulated, medical, legal, or revenue claims without approval.

## Experiment Shape

```json
{
  "hypothesis": "If we do X for audience Y, metric Z will move because evidence E.",
  "audience": "specific segment",
  "channel": "distribution channel",
  "asset": "copy, page, email, community post, SEO page, etc.",
  "evidence": ["source, user signal, search data, competitor example"],
  "successMetric": "primary metric",
  "minimumSignal": "what counts as enough data",
  "killCriterion": "when to stop",
  "risk": "LOW | MEDIUM | HIGH",
  "approvalRequired": "none | rafael | legal | medical"
}
```

## Review Topics

### GROW-AUDIENCE-EVIDENCE

Name who this is for and cite evidence that they exist and care.

### GROW-CLAIM-SAFETY

Check that copy does not overstate medical, research, financial, or AI reliability claims.

### GROW-MEASUREMENT

Define one primary metric and a practical measurement window.

### GROW-COST-CAP

State time, money, and agent budget before running.

## Required Output

1. Experiment hypothesis.
2. Evidence and assumptions.
3. Asset/task proposal.
4. Success and kill criteria.
5. Approval required before publication or spend.
